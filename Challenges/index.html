Wind Turbine Impact

In the last two decades, since the January 2001 earthquake, Kutch has enjoyed a major push in development and prosperity. This has been beneficial to people and industries, however, it has also become a threat to the biodiveristy of the region.

Kutch is considered ripe for development because it has lots of "empty lands". However, what may look barren and desolate in a quick glance is actually teeming with life. Over 500 species of animals and birds call Kutch their home, including migratory birds who come here to escape winters up north, and many to breed. Despite being an arid desert, Kutch is one of the most biologically diverse places you can find.

However, new industrialization drives, whether for clean energy or for production lines for various things, are dotting the landscape everywhere. Perhaps the square footage may not seem that large as after all Kutch has 45 thousand square kilometers to its name.
